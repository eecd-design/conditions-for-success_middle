## Webinar Overview

Every classroom and training room is filled with learners who bring different strengths, backgrounds, and ways of processing information. Universal Design for Learning (UDL) offers a research-based framework for designing instruction that anticipates this variability from the outset, rather than retrofitting accommodations after the fact. This live, interactive webinar introduces participants to the core philosophy behind UDL: that barriers to learning often live in the design of a lesson, not in the learner themselves. Through real classroom examples and guided discussion, attendees will come away with a clearer understanding of how to build flexibility into their teaching from day one.

## What You Will Learn

This session is built around the three core principles that anchor the UDL framework, as defined by CAST. By the end of the webinar, participants will be able to:

* Explain the three UDL principles: engagement, representation, and action/expression
* Identify common barriers to learning hidden within typical lesson design
* Generate practical ways to offer choice and flexibility in how content is taught and assessed
* Apply at least one UDL strategy to an upcoming lesson, training, or course module

The session balances conceptual grounding with immediately usable strategies, so participants leave with more than just theory.

## Session Format and Agenda

The webinar runs for 90 minutes and combines short presentations with interactive breakout discussions. The session opens with a brief overview of the origins and research base behind UDL, followed by a deeper dive into each of the three principles, illustrated with before-and-after examples from real lessons. Midway through, participants join small breakout groups to redesign a sample lesson using UDL principles, then reconvene to share insights with the larger group. The session closes with a live Q\&A, where the facilitator addresses specific challenges participants are facing in their own teaching contexts.

## Who Should Attend

This webinar is designed for anyone involved in designing or delivering instruction, regardless of subject area or experience level. It is especially relevant for:

* K-12 and higher education instructors looking to make lessons more accessible
* Instructional designers and curriculum developers
* Corporate trainers and L\&D professionals
* School and district leaders supporting inclusive teaching practices

No prior knowledge of UDL is required; the session is designed to be a welcoming entry point for newcomers while still offering useful refreshers for those with some background.

## Materials and Follow-Up

All registrants will receive a digital UDL planning template to use during the breakout activity and in their own future lesson design. A recording of the session, along with the presentation slides, will be shared with all registrants within 48 hours, regardless of attendance. Participants who wish to go deeper afterward will also receive a curated list of recommended readings and a self-paced lesson redesign worksheet to apply UDL principles to one of their own existing lessons.
